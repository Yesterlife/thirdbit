One-Day Workshop on How to Teach

A growing number of people who work in tech find themselves involved in teaching and training, either on the job as they mentor new hires or after hours as mentors in coding workshops and bootcamps. This intensive one-day workshop helps them do this better by introducing them to a handful of practical, evidence-based teaching practices. The workshop has been run over a hundred times in various forms on five continents for non-profit organizations, libraries, and companies, and all of the material is freely available online at http://teachtogether.tech. Topics include:

  • the differences between novices, competent practitioners, and experts;
  • mental models, cognitive load, and how to design learnable lessons;
  • teaching as a performance art;
  • six practices to accelerate learning; and
  • what motivates and demotivates adult learners.

Participants need to bring a laptop or tablet that can connect to the internet, a cell phone that can record a couple of minutes of audio and video, a few sheets of paper, and something to write with. For more information, please get in touch.
